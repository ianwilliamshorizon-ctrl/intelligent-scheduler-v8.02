# Standard Operating Procedure: Managing Jobs

**Objective:** To provide a clear and consistent workflow for managing jobs from creation to completion, ensuring that all work is tracked, technicians are assigned, and job statuses are kept up-to-date.

---

## 1. Job Creation

Jobs can be created in two primary ways:

*   **Converting an Estimate:** (See SOP-03 for details) This is the most common method. When an estimate is approved, it is converted into a job, carrying over all the details.
*   **Creating a Job Directly:**
    1.  Navigate to the **Jobs** section.
    2.  Click **"Create New Job"**.
    3.  You will need to manually select the **Customer** and **Vehicle**.
    4.  You can then add line items for parts and labor, similar to creating an estimate.
    5.  This method is best for small, on-the-spot jobs that don't require a formal estimate.

---

## 2. The Job Management Screen

Opening a job from the job list will display the main job management screen. This is a comprehensive view with several tabs:

*   **Estimate & Parts:** This tab shows the financial details of the job, including the parts and labor required. You can add or remove items here if necessary (e.g., for supplementary work). It also tracks the status of parts ordering.
*   **Inspection:** This tab contains the digital checklist for the vehicle inspection, including tyre checks and damage reports. Technicians fill this out during the service.
*   **Technician Notes:** A dedicated area for technicians to add observations, notes, and photos/videos related to the job.
*   **Segments:** This tab shows the scheduled work segments for the job, including the date, time, allocated lift, and assigned engineer.

---

## 3. The Job Lifecycle and Status

A job progresses through several statuses, which are updated automatically or manually as work is completed.

*   **Booked In:** The initial status when a job is created and scheduled.
*   **Work In Progress:** The job is actively being worked on by a technician.
*   **Quality Control:** The work is complete, and the job is awaiting a final quality check.
*   **Awaiting Invoice:** The work is done and has passed QC. The job is now ready to be invoiced.
*   **Invoiced:** An invoice has been generated for the job. The job becomes read-only.
*   **Completed:** The invoice has been paid, and the job is fully closed.
*   **Cancelled:** The job has been cancelled and will be kept in the system for historical purposes.

### Key Actions:

*   **Check-In / Check-Out:** When a vehicle arrives at the workshop, use the **"Check-In"** button to record its arrival. Use **"Check-Out"** when the customer collects the vehicle.
*   **Scheduling:** Use the main **Schedule** view to drag and drop jobs onto technician and lift timelines, creating the work segments.
*   **Parts Ordering:** If parts are required, you can raise a **Purchase Order** directly from the job screen.

---

## 4. Invoicing a Job

1.  Once a job is in the **"Awaiting Invoice"** status, open the job.
2.  Click the **"Create Invoice"** button.
3.  The system will generate a new invoice, pulling all the billable items from the job.
4.  You will be taken to the invoice screen to review and send the invoice to the customer.

---

**Best Practices:**

*   **Real-Time Updates:** Technicians and service advisors should update job details and statuses in real-time to ensure everyone has the most current information.
*   **Use the Inspection Checklists:** Ensure the digital inspection is completed for every job to maintain a high standard of quality and a complete vehicle history.
*   **Clear Technician Notes:** Encourage technicians to take clear photos and write detailed notes. This is invaluable for justifying work to customers and for future reference.
